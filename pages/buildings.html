On Campus Housing

At BYU, you can live in one of two on campus housing options: Heritage Halls or Helaman Halls

This is Helaman Halls. In Helaman Halls, each resident gets a bed, desk and wardrobe. If you live here, you'll also need a mealplan and eat your meals in the Cannon Center. You live with one roommate and share a large bathroom with the other people on your floor.

This is Heriage Halls. In Heritage Halls, each resident gets a bed, desk and wardrobe. Residents here live in an appartment with 4-6 people. People who live here generally make their own food. Each appartment is fully furnished and has a kitchen and small living area.

The Eyering science center is home to a planeterium as well as a science museuem.

The HFAC or Franklin S Harris Fine Arts Center is home to the school of Music and Communications. Classes such as choir, band, orchestra and art would be found here. This building is also home to the De Jong Concert Hall.

The Maeser Building (MSRB) is named for Karl G Maeser, the principal and spiritual architect of Brigham Young Academy. The building is the oldest on campus and currently houses the honors program

The Thomas L Martin Classroom Building (MARB) was built in 1968 to honor a former dean. The building is home to several large lecture halls and classrooms. Classes from nearly every college can be found here.

The LSB or Life Science Building. Completed in 2014, the LSB is one of the newest buildings on campus. It is home to many life science classes such as biology, health science and microbiology.

THe Herald R Clark Building (HRCB) is currently home to the Kennedy center. The Kennedy center is home to the BYU study abroad program.

This 12 story tower is named after Spencer W Kimball. Kimball Tower (KMBL) is home to the College of Family Home and Social Sciences and the College of Nursing.

The Joseph F. Smith Building (JFSB) is home to the College of Humanities. It also houses the Center for Language Studies.
